## Rationale notes

- **v1.4.18 carryover (5 issues) accepted into v1.4.19** rather than spread across v1.4.19 + v1.4.20: the items are tightly coupled to ongoing reader-uplift and data-layer work, and the v1.4.18 milestone-as-tag is closed. Re-spreading would create the same milestone-vs-tag drift Retro v1.4.17 named.
- **#541 + #328 paired in v1.4.19** because the consistency-assertion scope of #541 covers part of the temporal-coupling pattern in #328. Fixing one without the other risks the assertion not catching the cluster #328 names. Either both close together, or #328 splits during execution.
- **Verify-strands all in parallel auto-mode** per the v1.4.18 retro learning ("research / verification / code-refactor / infra suits auto-mode; content drafting is always paced"). Briefs are ready; cross-strand collisions are forecast in each brief's §7.
- **Seg 12 drafting deadline is tight** (Wed 2026-05-13 publication, brief spawned Tue 2026-05-12 evening). Publisher-paced; the strand session owns its own checkpoints.
- **Why milestone closure is targeted at W21-seg14**: the milestone title says "segs 14-16 prep," and seg 14 publication is the first time that prep work hits production. Closing the milestone earlier (after W20-seg13) would be premature — segs 14-16 data verification needs to land before seg 14's drafting strand can run cleanly. Closing it later (e.g. after W21-seg14 itself) would conflate prep with publication; the publication retro should be its own scope.
- **Three issues without sibling-strand briefs** (#466 map render, #483 instrumentation, #535 entry-card thumbnails) need their own scope decisions when they're picked up. Recommended order: #535 first (smallest, EntryCard already extracted), then #466 (map performance has clearer success criteria than #483), then #483 (needs instrumentation-tool decision first).
