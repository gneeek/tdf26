- **Geological-transition story deferred from seg 11.** The seg 11 draft originally framed the Côte de Naves as the lithological boundary between the Brive sedimentary basin / Jurassic limestones and the Variscan granite of the Limousin basement. Pre-publish scrutiny against BRGM Tulle 761 + Université de Limoges geology sources showed this is wrong: the Brive basin is confined to ~600 km² in southwestern Corrèze, and its boundary fault system runs through the *southwestern corner of the Tulle map sheet* — well south of Tulle. The route has been on Massif Central basement / metamorphics since roughly Beynat (seg 5/6); Tulle itself sits on the Tulle anticline with granodioritic plutons in its core; the Côte de Naves is a **topographic** threshold (river valley → plateau), not a lithological one. Seg 11 ¶3 was reduced to a one-line "granite plateau ahead" framing and the lithological transition story removed. Pick the geological-boundary narrative up in a later segment where the geology actually changes — natural homes: a Plateau de Millevaches segment (18-20), or Mont Bessou (seg 22). Sources to reuse: [BRGM Tulle 761 notice](http://ficheinfoterre.brgm.fr/Notices/0761N.pdf), [Bassin sédimentaire de Brive (Université de Limoges)](https://www.unilim.fr/musee_geologique_de_plein_air/geologie-du-limousin/le-bassin-sedimentaire-de-brive/), [Géologie du Limousin (FR Wikipédia)](https://fr.wikipedia.org/wiki/G%C3%A9ologie_du_Limousin).
